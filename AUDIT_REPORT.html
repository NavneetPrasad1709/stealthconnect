Critical — Fixed Immediately
✓
C1
Sign-up page had the wrong title in the browser tab
When someone opened the sign-up page, their browser tab said "Sign In" instead of "Create your account." This confused users with multiple tabs open and also hurt our Google ranking for signup-related searches. Now fixed — the tab shows the correct title.
✓
C2
Pricing calculator showed a different price than our own pricing table
A customer buying 50 phone numbers would see two different prices on the same page — the calculator said one thing, the pricing table said another. This would cause billing disputes and destroy trust. Both are now in sync and show the same number at every quantity.
✓
C3
Testimonials used stock photos and fake quotes from Salesforce, HubSpot, and Stripe employees
All 9 customer testimonials had free stock photos as profile pictures and were attributed to employees of real companies like Salesforce, HubSpot, Stripe, Notion, and Intercom — none of whom are actual customers. This is illegal under US (FTC), EU, and UK consumer protection law. Any visitor doing a reverse image search would immediately see the photos are fake, destroying credibility. All fake attributions and stock photos have been removed and replaced with neutral placeholders.
High Priority — Fixed
✓
H1
Our accuracy stat appeared as 5 different numbers across the same website
Visitors could see 99.9%, 97.4%, 97.2%, and 97% all on the same page. This looks careless and untrustworthy. We also removed the 99.9% figure — it is implausibly high for this type of service and could attract regulatory scrutiny. Now a single number, 97.2%, is used everywhere.
✓
H2
The homepage search bar looked interactive but ignored what users typed
Our hero section shows a LinkedIn URL input with a "Find Contact" button. Whatever the user typed was thrown away — clicking the button always just went to the sign-up page with no memory of what they entered. This feels like a trick. Now the typed URL is carried forward to the sign-up page with a note saying "We'll look this up for free once you sign in."
✓
H3
The login page was invisible to Google and link previews showed a blank page
When someone shared our login page link on Slack or LinkedIn, the preview was completely empty. Google also couldn't read the page. Fixed — the page now loads properly for everyone, including search engines and preview bots. The browser tab title was also showing the brand name twice ("StealthConnect AI | StealthConnect AI") — that's fixed too.
✓
H4
We published 4 different contact email addresses with no explanation of which to use
Customers found hello@, support@, sales@, and privacy@ scattered across different pages. There was no guidance on which to use for billing, account issues, or data requests. Standardised to one public address: support@stealthconnect.ai. Legal-specific addresses (privacy@, gdpr@) kept only where required by law.
✓
H5
No company name, address, or registration number anywhere on the site
EU law requires every online business to display its legal company name, registered address, and company number. Enterprise buyers also require this for vendor due-diligence. A disclosure block has been added to the footer. Action needed: fill in your actual company name, country, and registration number.
✓
H6
Our Terms of Service said "governed by the laws of our jurisdiction" but never named a jurisdiction
A governing-law clause without a named country is essentially unenforceable in court. Structured placeholders have been added. Action needed: have your lawyer fill in the actual country and dispute process.
✓
H7
Profile photos were loading at 40× the size needed, slowing the page down
Each testimonial photo was being downloaded at full camera resolution just to display as a small thumbnail. This wasted bandwidth and slowed the page for every visitor. Resolved as a side effect of fixing C3 — profile photos are now CSS-generated initials with zero download cost.

Medium Priority — Fixed
✓
M1 & M7
Login and sign-up pages had wrong or duplicate titles in Google search results
The login page title showed "StealthConnect AI | StealthConnect AI" — the brand name twice. The sign-up page title said "Sign In." Both are now correct and distinct.
✓
M2
Google could not read our FAQ answers — only the questions were indexed
Our FAQ section only showed answers after a user clicked to expand them. Google's search crawler never saw the answers. This meant we were missing out on appearing in search results when people type our FAQ questions directly. The answers are now always present on the page — just visually hidden until clicked — so Google indexes all of them.
✓
M3
Most pages had generic or missing descriptions in Google search results
The text that appears under our page title in Google was either missing or too vague to drive clicks. Unique, descriptive summaries have been written for every public page.
✓
M4
Google had no structured information about our product, pricing, or FAQ
Without structured data, Google cannot show our FAQ as expandable dropdowns in search results, or display our pricing in product cards. We've added the required markup — this makes us eligible for richer, more prominent Google listings.
✓
M5
"GDPR" badge on the homepage implied an official certification that doesn't exist
GDPR is a law, not a certificate. No authority issues a "GDPR certified" badge. Displaying it as one is misleading to customers. Changed to "GDPR-aware" and linked to our GDPR page which explains our actual approach. Our features section also used "GDPR Compliant" as a label — updated to "GDPR-Aware" with clearer, factual wording.
✓
M6
Screen readers for visually impaired users were announcing "bullet" before every section heading
Every section on our site uses a small decorative dot (●) before the heading text. Assistive technology was reading this out loud as "bullet How It Works", "bullet Pricing", etc. — confusing and unprofessional for users relying on screen readers. One small code change hides the dot from assistive technology while keeping it visible on screen.
✓
M8
Google didn't know which version of our URLs to treat as the "official" one
Without canonical URL tags, search engines can get confused between www/non-www versions of pages and split ranking credit. Canonical tags have been added to all pages, telling Google exactly which URL to index.
✓
M9
Contact page had no form — only email links that don't work on phones without a mail app
Anyone on a phone without a mail app set up could not contact us at all. A simple Name / Email / Message form has been added. Messages are delivered directly to our support inbox. Action needed: add the email API key to the server so the form can send messages.
Low Priority — Fixed
✓
